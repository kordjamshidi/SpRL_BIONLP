Bartonella quintana str. Toulouse Toulose
Description
Bartonella. This group of alpha proteobacteria are unique among pathogens in that they cause angiogenic lesions. Angiogenesis is the process of creating new blood vessels.

Bartonella quintana. This organism was first identified as the causative agent of louse-born trench fever during World War I. Up to 800 000 were infected in the trenches because of the louse insect vector that was widespread due to poor sanitation. Characterized by fever, rashes, bone pain, and enlargement of the spleen. The disease is now found associated with immunocompromized patients, especially HIV patients and homeless alcoholics.
